# Density-dependent simplified Example 10 of the PHT3D Manual

This example is a density-dependent simplifyied version of Example 10 from the PHT3D documentation {cite}`PHT3D`.

```{epigraph}
Toluene enters from the left into a homogeous aquifer with parallel flow from left to right. The Toluene plume exhibits also a high salinity of 35 g/L and thus has a 
higher density (1025 kg/m3) than the uncontaminated water (1000 kg/m3). Toluene degrades via 1st order decay consuming various electron acceptors. The model simulates the corresponding terminal electron acceptor processes and some secondary reactions.
The contamination in the inflowing groundwater stops after day 375 and uncontaminated groundwater enters the aquifer over the whole depth for the remaining 125 days.
```
